Chapter 13: Payment of Tax

Understanding GST Tax Payment Procedures and Mechanisms

← Previous Chapter: E-Way Bill Next Chapter: TDS and TCS under GST →

Introduction to GST Tax Payment

Payment of tax under GST is a critical compliance requirement where registered persons must discharge their tax liability by making payments to the government within prescribed time limits. The system ensures timely collection of revenue and maintains the flow of input tax credit throughout the supply chain.

Definition and Importance

Tax payment under GST refers to the process of depositing the calculated tax liability (output tax minus input tax credit) with the government through authorized channels. It is essential for maintaining compliance, avoiding interest and penalties, and ensuring smooth business operations.

Legal Framework

Tax payment provisions are governed by Sections 45 to 51 of the CGST Act, 2017:

  • Section 45: Deposit of tax collected in electronic form
  • Section 46: Utilization of input tax credit
  • Section 47: Manner of payment of tax
  • Section 48: Interest on delayed payment
  • Section 49: Payment of tax in certain cases
  • Section 50: Recovery of tax
  • Section 51: Tax deduction at source

Key Points to Remember

  • Tax must be paid in electronic form only
  • Payment due by 20th of next month for monthly filers
  • IGST paid first, then CGST, then SGST/UTGST
  • Interest @ 18% per annum for delayed payments
  • Proper accounting and reconciliation required

Tax Payment Liability

Registered persons are liable to pay tax on:

  • Output tax on outward supplies
  • Tax on reverse charge mechanism supplies
  • Tax on supplies made through e-commerce platforms
  • Tax on import of goods and services
  • Interest, penalties, and other amounts

Methods of Tax Payment

Electronic Cash Ledger

Main account for tax payments. All deposits made through this ledger. Maintained separately for CGST, SGST, IGST, and Cess.

Electronic Credit Ledger

Account for input tax credit. ITC automatically credited and can be used for tax payments.

Electronic Liability Register

Tracks tax liabilities as per returns filed. Payments made against these liabilities.

Payment Process

Step-by-Step Payment Process

  1. Calculate Tax Liability: Determine output tax, ITC, and net tax payable
  2. Deposit Funds: Transfer money to electronic cash ledger through authorized banks
  3. File Returns: GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, or other applicable returns
  4. Create Challan: Generate challan in GST portal for tax payment
  5. Make Payment: Pay using net banking, credit/debit card, or NEFT/RTGS
  6. Generate CPIN: Common Portal Identification Number generated after payment
  7. Update Ledger: Payment reflected in electronic cash ledger
  8. Utilize Credit: Use ITC from electronic credit ledger for payments

Payment Due Dates

Taxpayer Category Due Date for Payment
Regular Taxpayers (Monthly) By 20th of the next month
Regular Taxpayers (Quarterly) By 20th of the month following the quarter
Composition Dealers By 18th of the month following the quarter
E-commerce Operators (TCS) By 10th of the next month
TDS Deductors By 10th of the next month

Tax Payment Scenarios

  • Regular Supplier: Company with output tax ₹50,000 and ITC ₹30,000. Net payable ₹20,000 by 20th of next month.
  • Reverse Charge: Dealer receiving goods from unregistered supplier worth ₹1,00,000. Pays 18% GST ₹18,000 on reverse charge basis.
  • E-commerce Operator: Marketplace collecting TCS at 1% on supplies. Pays TCS by 10th of next month.
  • Import: Importer paying IGST on imported goods at the time of customs clearance.

Interest on Delayed Payments

Interest is levied for delayed tax payments:

  • Rate: 18% per annum on outstanding tax amount
  • Calculation: Simple interest from due date till actual payment date
  • Minimum Period: Interest calculated for minimum 1 month even if delay is less
  • Recovery: Automatically calculated and must be paid along with tax

Authorized Payment Methods

Tax payments can be made through the following channels:

  • Net Banking: Through authorized banks with GST integration
  • Credit/Debit Cards: Via GST portal with applicable charges
  • NEFT/RTGS: Electronic fund transfer through banks
  • Over the Counter: Through authorized bank branches
  • E-payment: Through GST portal using various payment gateways

Tax Payment Reconciliation

Proper reconciliation is essential for accurate compliance:

  • Monthly reconciliation of cash ledger with bank statements
  • Matching of tax paid with liabilities reported in returns
  • Verification of ITC utilization from credit ledger
  • Reconciliation of challan status in GST portal
  • Resolution of any discrepancies or mismatches

Common Payment Issues

Businesses commonly face these tax payment challenges:

  • Delay in fund transfers leading to interest liability
  • Technical issues with GST portal during payment
  • Mismatch between challan generation and bank realization
  • Incorrect utilization of ITC from credit ledger
  • Non-receipt of CPIN after payment

Recent Amendments and Updates

The GST Council has made several updates to payment provisions:

  • Simplified payment procedures for small taxpayers
  • Enhanced mobile payment options
  • Improved challan tracking mechanisms
  • Special provisions for MSMEs
  • Streamlined interest calculation and payment

Conclusion

Timely and accurate payment of GST is fundamental to maintaining compliance and avoiding interest and penalties. Understanding the payment mechanisms, due dates, and reconciliation procedures ensures smooth business operations and financial management. Regular monitoring of tax liabilities, proper fund planning, and implementation of systematic payment processes help businesses meet their obligations efficiently. Staying updated with payment procedure changes and leveraging technology for automated payment tracking contribute to effective GST compliance and financial discipline.
