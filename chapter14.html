Chapter 14: TDS and TCS under GST

Understanding Tax Deducted at Source and Tax Collected at Source

← Previous Chapter: Payment of Tax Next Chapter: GST Returns →

Introduction to TDS and TCS under GST

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) and Tax Collected at Source (TCS) are mechanisms under GST where tax is collected at the point of transaction by designated entities. These provisions ensure better compliance and wider tax base by bringing more entities into the tax net.

Definition and Purpose

TDS and TCS under GST are compliance mechanisms where specified categories of persons deduct or collect tax at prescribed rates from payments made to suppliers or collected from recipients. This ensures timely collection of tax and prevents tax evasion by bringing government departments, PSUs, and e-commerce operators into the tax collection system.

Legal Framework

TDS and TCS provisions are governed by Sections 51 and 52 of the CGST Act, 2017:

  • Section 51: Tax deduction at source
  • Section 52: Tax collection at source
  • Section 53: Furnishing of information by deductor and collector
  • Section 54: Refund of tax deducted or collected
  • Rule 66-71: Rules related to TDS and TCS under CGST Rules, 2017

Key Points to Remember

  • TDS @ 1% (0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST/UTGST) or 2% IGST
  • TCS @ 1% (0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST/UTGST) on e-commerce supplies
  • Threshold of ₹2.5 lakhs for TDS applicability
  • Must deposit within 10 days of next month
  • Issue TDS certificate within 5 days of deduction

Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)

TDS under GST is deducted by specified entities at the time of making payments to suppliers:

Entities Required to Deduct TDS

  • Departments or establishments of Central or State Government
  • Local authorities
  • Governmental agencies
  • Such persons or categories as may be notified by the GST Council

TDS Rates and Threshold

Type of Supply TDS Rate Threshold
Intra-State Supply 1% (0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST/UTGST) ₹2.5 lakhs
Inter-State Supply 2% IGST ₹2.5 lakhs

TDS Process and Requirements

Registration

Entities required to deduct TDS must obtain separate TDS registration with PAN-based GSTIN.

Deduction

Deduct tax at prescribed rates when payment exceeds threshold limit to registered suppliers.

Deposit

Deposit deducted tax with government within 10 days of the next month through challan.

Filing

File GSTR-7 monthly return and furnish details of TDS deducted and deposited.

Tax Collected at Source (TCS)

TCS under GST is collected by e-commerce operators at the time of supplying goods or services through their platform:

Entities Required to Collect TCS

  • E-commerce operators who facilitate supply of goods or services
  • Operators of digital platforms facilitating supplies
  • Marketplace facilitators

TCS Rates and Applicability

TCS is collected at the rate of:

  • 1%: 0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST/UTGST for intra-state supplies
  • 1%: 1% IGST for inter-state supplies

TDS and TCS Scenarios

  • Government Department: Central Government department pays ₹5 lakhs to a registered supplier. Deducts TDS @ 2% (₹10,000) on inter-state supply.
  • E-commerce Operator: Amazon facilitates sale of ₹1,00,000 worth goods. Collects TCS @ 1% (₹1,000) from customer.
  • Local Authority: Municipal corporation pays ₹3 lakhs for construction work. Deducts TDS @ 1% (₹3,000) on intra-state supply.
  • PSU: BHEL purchases goods worth ₹4 lakhs. Deducts TDS @ 2% (₹8,000) on inter-state supply.

TDS and TCS Comparison

Aspect TDS (Section 51) TCS (Section 52)
Who Deducts/Collects Government departments, PSUs, local authorities E-commerce operators
When Applied At the time of making payment to supplier At the time of collecting payment from recipient
Rate 1% or 2% depending on supply type 1% for both intra and inter-state
Threshold ₹2.5 lakhs per contract No threshold limit
Return Filing GSTR-7 monthly GSTR-8 monthly

Compliance Requirements

Both TDS and TCS deductors/collectors must comply with:

  • Obtain separate registration for TDS/TCS activities
  • Deduct/collect tax at prescribed rates and times
  • Deposit tax with government within prescribed time limits
  • File monthly returns (GSTR-7 for TDS, GSTR-8 for TCS)
  • Issue certificates to suppliers/recipients
  • Maintain proper records and accounts

Certificates and Documentation

Proper documentation is essential for TDS and TCS compliance:

  • TDS Certificate: Form GSTR-7A issued within 5 days of deduction
  • TCS Certificate: Form GSTR-8A issued within 5 days of collection
  • Payment Challan: CPIN generated for tax deposit
  • Return Filing: Monthly returns with details of deduction/collection
  • Record Maintenance: Proper books of accounts for all transactions

Input Tax Credit for TDS/TCS

Suppliers and recipients can claim credit for TDS/TCS:

  • TDS amount reflected in Form GSTR-7A is eligible for ITC
  • TCS amount reflected in Form GSTR-8A is eligible for ITC
  • Credit automatically populated in electronic credit ledger
  • Matching with deductor/collector returns required
  • Mismatch results in ITC reversal

Common Compliance Issues

Entities commonly face these TDS/TCS challenges:

  • Delay in obtaining separate TDS/TCS registration
  • Incorrect calculation of TDS/TCS amounts
  • Delay in depositing deducted/collected tax
  • Non-issuance of certificates within prescribed time
  • Errors in return filing and reconciliation

Recent Amendments and Updates

The GST Council has made several updates to TDS/TCS provisions:

  • Simplified registration process for TDS deductors
  • Enhanced auto-population in returns
  • Special provisions for small e-commerce operators
  • Improved certificate generation mechanisms
  • Streamlined refund processes for excess deduction

Conclusion

TDS and TCS provisions under GST are important compliance mechanisms that expand the tax base and ensure better tax collection. Understanding the requirements, rates, and procedures for both deductors and collectors is essential for proper compliance. Regular training on TDS/TCS provisions, implementation of systematic processes, and timely filing of returns help entities meet their obligations efficiently. The mechanisms not only aid government revenue collection but also provide legitimate ITC benefits to suppliers and recipients, making them integral to the GST ecosystem.
