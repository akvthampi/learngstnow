Chapter 9: Registration

Understanding GST Registration Requirements and Procedures

← Previous Chapter: Input Tax Credit Next Chapter: Tax Invoice, Credit Notes and Debit Notes →

Introduction to GST Registration

GST Registration is the process by which businesses obtain a unique identification number under the Goods and Services Tax regime. It is a mandatory requirement for entities that meet the prescribed turnover thresholds or fall under specific categories as defined in the GST law.

Definition and Importance

Registration under GST is the legal process of enrolling a business under the GST regime, which enables the entity to collect tax on behalf of the government, claim input tax credit, and comply with various GST obligations. It is the foundation for all GST-related activities.

Legal Framework

Registration provisions are governed by Sections 22 to 30 of the CGST Act, 2017:

  • Section 22: Liability to registration
  • Section 23: Persons not liable for registration
  • Section 24: Persons liable to take registration
  • Section 25: Application for registration
  • Section 26: Grant of registration
  • Section 27: Amendment of registration
  • Section 28: Cancellation of registration
  • Section 29: Revocation of cancellation
  • Section 30: Persons deducting tax at source to obtain registration

Key Points to Remember

  • Registration is mandatory for businesses above threshold limits
  • Voluntary registration is available for businesses below limits
  • Each state/UT requires separate registration
  • Unique GSTIN is issued for each registration
  • Proper documentation is essential for registration

Liability to Registration

Entities are liable for GST registration based on turnover thresholds:

  • Goods: Aggregate turnover exceeds ₹40 lakhs (₹20 lakhs for special category states)
  • Services: Aggregate turnover exceeds ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs for special category states)
  • Inter-State Supplies: Registration required irrespective of turnover
  • E-commerce Operators: Mandatory registration irrespective of turnover

Mandatory Registration Categories

Certain categories must register irrespective of turnover:

E-commerce Operators

All e-commerce operators and suppliers through e-commerce platforms must register regardless of turnover.

Casual Taxable Persons

Persons occasionally making taxable supplies in a state where they have no fixed place of business.

Non-resident Taxable Persons

Persons making taxable supplies in India without a permanent establishment in the country.

Input Service Distributors

Offices receiving tax invoices for input services and distributing credit to branches.

Registration Process

Step-by-Step Registration Process

  1. Access GST Portal: Visit www.gst.gov.in and click on 'Services' > 'User Services' > 'Registration'
  2. New Registration: Select 'New Registration' and choose the appropriate category
  3. Temporary Reference Number (TRN): Enter PAN and mobile/email to generate TRN
  4. OTP Verification: Verify mobile number and email through OTP
  5. Application Form: Fill Part A and Part B of Form GST REG-01
  6. Document Upload: Upload required documents as per checklist
  7. Submission: Submit application and receive Application Reference Number (ARN)
  8. Verification: Physical verification if required by tax authorities
  9. Certificate Issuance: GST Registration Certificate issued upon approval

Documents Required for Registration

Common documents required for GST registration:

  • PAN Card of the business entity
  • Aadhaar Card of authorized signatory
  • Proof of business registration/incorporation
  • Proof of principal place of business (ownership/rent agreement)
  • Bank account details and cancelled cheque
  • Photograph of authorized signatory
  • Digital Signature Certificate (for companies/LLPs)
  • Additional documents for specific categories (e.g., import-export code for exporters)

Registration Scenarios

  • Manufacturer: Company manufacturing goods with turnover ₹50 lakhs must register in each state of operation.
  • Service Provider: Consultant with turnover ₹25 lakhs must register in each state where services are provided.
  • E-commerce Seller: Seller on Amazon with turnover ₹5 lakhs must register irrespective of threshold.
  • Inter-State Supplier: Delhi-based supplier making sales to Mumbai must register in both states.

Types of Registration

Registration Type Description Applicability
Regular Registration Standard registration for businesses above threshold Most common type for regular businesses
Composition Scheme Simplified scheme for small businesses Turnover up to ₹1.5 crores
Casual Registration For occasional business activities Temporary business in a state
Non-resident Registration For foreign entities making supplies Non-resident taxable persons
Input Service Distributor For distributing input tax credit Offices receiving input services

Unique Features of GST Registration

Key features that distinguish GST registration:

  • GSTIN: 15-digit unique identification number
  • State-wise Registration: Separate registration for each state/UT
  • Automatic Migration: Existing taxpayers migrated automatically
  • Digital Process: Entirely online registration process
  • Centralized Database: Single database for all registrations

Amendment and Cancellation

Provisions for changes to registration:

  • Amendment: Changes to business details through Form GST REG-14
  • Cancellation: Voluntary or compulsory cancellation of registration
  • Revocation: Revocation of cancelled registration within 30 days
  • Surrender: Surrender of GSTIN upon business closure

Common Registration Issues

Businesses commonly face these registration challenges:

  • Delay in approval due to document verification
  • Incorrect GSTIN or business details
  • Multiple registrations for same business
  • Non-receipt of login credentials
  • Technical issues with GST portal

Recent Amendments and Updates

The GST Council has made several updates to registration provisions:

  • Simplified registration process for MSMEs
  • Enhanced auto-population of data
  • Improved grievance redressal mechanisms
  • Special provisions for startups
  • Streamlined cancellation and revocation procedures

Conclusion

Registration under GST is the gateway to the tax system and a prerequisite for conducting business in the GST regime. Understanding the eligibility criteria, registration process, and compliance requirements is essential for businesses to operate smoothly. Proper documentation, timely application, and accurate information ensure quick approval and hassle-free registration. Businesses must also stay updated with changes in registration provisions and maintain their registration details accurately to avoid compliance issues. Regular review of registration requirements helps businesses adapt to changing circumstances and maintain proper GST compliance.
