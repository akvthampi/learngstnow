Chapter 6: Time of Supply

Determining When GST Liability Arises on Taxable Supplies

← Previous Chapter: Exemptions from GST Next Chapter: Value of Supply →

Introduction to Time of Supply

The time of supply is a critical concept in GST that determines when the liability to pay tax arises on a taxable supply. It establishes the point at which GST becomes payable to the government, regardless of when the actual payment is received or when the invoice is issued.

Definition and Importance

Time of supply refers to the specific point in time when a taxable supply is deemed to have taken place for the purpose of determining GST liability. It is crucial for proper tax compliance, timely payment, and accurate reporting in GST returns.

Legal Framework

The time of supply is governed by Sections 12 and 13 of the CGST Act, 2017:

  • Section 12: Time of supply for goods
  • Section 13: Time of supply for services
  • Section 14: Time of supply in case of supply of vouchers
  • Section 15: Determination of value (related to time of supply)

Key Points to Remember

  • Time of supply determines when GST liability arises
  • Different rules apply for goods and services
  • Earlier event rule is applied to determine time of supply
  • Special rules exist for reverse charge mechanism
  • Proper determination is crucial for compliance and ITC claims

Time of Supply for Goods

For supply of goods, the time of supply is the earlier of the following:

  • Date of issue of invoice by the supplier
  • Date of receipt of payment by the supplier

Specific Rules for Goods

  • If invoice is not issued within the prescribed time, time of supply is the date of completion of supply
  • If payment is received before issue of invoice, time of supply is the date of receipt of payment
  • For continuous supply of goods, time of supply is determined based on periodicity of supply

Time of Supply for Services

For supply of services, the time of supply is the earlier of the following:

  • Date of issue of invoice by the supplier
  • Date of receipt of payment by the supplier

Special Cases for Services

  • If invoice is not issued within the prescribed time, time of supply is the date of completion of service
  • For continuous supply of services, time of supply is determined based on periodicity of supply
  • For services related to immovable property, time of supply is when payment is received

Prescribed Time Limits for Invoice Issuance

Type of Supply Time Limit for Invoice
Goods (normal) Before or at the time of removal of goods
Services (normal) Before or after provision of service but within 30 days
Banking/Financial Services Within 45 days from date of supply
Continuous Supply of Goods Before or at the time of issue of statement/periodic return
Continuous Supply of Services Before or at the time of issue of statement/periodic return

Special Cases and Scenarios

Reverse Charge Mechanism

Time of supply is the earliest of: date of receipt of goods, date of payment, or date immediately following 30 days from supplier's invoice date.

Vouchers

Time of supply is the date of redemption of voucher or expiry of validity period, whichever is earlier.

Composite Supply

Time of supply is determined based on the principal supply in the composite supply.

Practical Examples

  • Goods Supply: A supplier delivers goods on March 15 and issues invoice on March 20. Payment received on March 18. Time of supply is March 15 (earlier of invoice date or payment date).
  • Services: A consultant completes service on April 10 and issues invoice on April 15. Payment received on April 12. Time of supply is April 10 (earlier of invoice date or payment date).
  • Reverse Charge: A registered dealer receives goods on May 5 and pays on May 10. Supplier's invoice date is May 8. Time of supply is May 5 (date of receipt of goods).

Continuous Supply

Special rules apply for continuous supply of goods or services:

  • Fixed Periodicity: Time of supply is the date of issue of statement/periodic return
  • Variable Periodicity: Time of supply is the date of completion of service
  • Payment Linked: Time of supply is the date of receipt of payment

Time of Supply for Vouchers

For vouchers, the time of supply is determined as follows:

  • If the supply is identifiable at the time of issuance: Time of supply is the time of issuance
  • If the supply is not identifiable: Time of supply is the time of redemption
  • If voucher expires without redemption: Time of supply is the date of expiry

Time of Supply in Case of Addition in Value

When the taxable value is enhanced by way of interest, penalty, or any other amount:

  • Time of supply is the date on which such additional amount is collected
  • Applies to both goods and services
  • Must be declared in the return for the month in which collected

Documentation Requirements

Proper documentation is essential for determining time of supply:

  • Invoices with proper dates
  • Payment receipts and bank statements
  • Delivery challans and transport documents
  • Service completion certificates
  • Contracts and agreements

Common Challenges and Issues

Businesses often face challenges in determining time of supply:

  • Delay in invoice issuance beyond prescribed time limits
  • Discrepancies between invoice date and payment date
  • Complexity in continuous supply arrangements
  • Proper documentation for reverse charge supplies
  • Timing issues in voucher redemptions

Impact on GST Compliance

Correct determination of time of supply affects:

  • Timely payment of GST liability
  • Proper reporting in GST returns
  • Availment of input tax credit
  • Interest liability on delayed payments
  • Compliance with audit requirements

Recent Amendments and Updates

The GST Council has made several clarifications on time of supply:

  • Simplified rules for continuous supply
  • Clarifications on voucher supply timing
  • Extended time limits for certain services
  • Special provisions for digital services

Conclusion

Understanding the time of supply is fundamental to GST compliance as it determines when tax liability arises and when GST must be paid to the government. Businesses must establish clear procedures for invoice issuance, payment collection, and documentation to ensure accurate determination of time of supply. Proper compliance with time of supply provisions helps avoid interest liabilities, penalties, and disputes with tax authorities. Regular training of staff and implementation of robust accounting systems ensure timely and accurate GST compliance under the GST regime.
