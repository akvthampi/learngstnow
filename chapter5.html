Chapter 5: Exemptions from GST

Understanding Goods and Services Exempt from GST Liability

← Previous Chapter: Place of Supply Next Chapter: Time of Supply →

Introduction to GST Exemptions

Exemptions from GST refer to supplies on which no tax is payable under the GST law. These exemptions are crucial for reducing the tax burden on essential goods and services, promoting certain sectors, and ensuring social welfare. Understanding exemptions is essential for businesses to identify tax-free supplies and for proper compliance.

Definition of Exemption

An exemption under GST means that certain supplies are either not subject to tax (nil rated) or are wholly exempt from payment of tax. In both cases, no GST is charged on such supplies, but the treatment of input tax credit differs between nil rated and exempt supplies.

Types of Exemptions

Exemptions under GST can be classified into different categories:

Nil Rated Supplies

Supplies on which GST rate is notified as nil. No tax is payable, but ITC on inputs can be availed.

Wholly Exempt Supplies

Supplies specifically exempted through notifications. No tax is payable and ITC on inputs is reversed.

Conditional Exemptions

Exemptions available subject to fulfillment of certain conditions or thresholds.

Legal Framework for Exemptions

Exemptions under GST are governed by specific provisions:

  • Section 11: Power to grant exemption from payment of tax
  • Schedule III: Activities treated as neither supply of goods nor services
  • Schedule IV: Services by government entities treated as supply
  • Notifications: Specific exemptions notified by the GST Council

Key Points to Remember

  • Nil rated supplies have 0% GST rate but allow ITC
  • Exempt supplies have no tax but reverse ITC
  • Exemptions can be general or specific
  • Exemptions may be conditional or unconditional
  • Proper classification is crucial for compliance and ITC treatment

Specific Exemptions for Goods

Numerous goods are exempt from GST under various notifications:

  • Agricultural Produce: Fresh fruits, vegetables, milk, eggs
  • Food Items: Salt, jaggery, unbranded paneer
  • Books and Publications: Textbooks, newspapers, journals
  • Medical Supplies: Human blood, contraceptives
  • Educational Materials: Charts, maps, globes
  • Religious Items: Religious books, idols

Specific Exemptions for Services

Various services are exempt from GST to promote social welfare:

  • Healthcare: Clinical services, veterinary services
  • Education: Pre-school, school, higher education
  • Public Services: Municipality services, public transportation
  • Financial: Account opening, cheque collection
  • Employment: Job placement services
  • Cultural: Circus, puppet shows, folk dances

Practical Examples

  • Nil Rated: Supply of wheat by a farmer to a wholesaler - GST rate is 0%, but wholesaler can claim ITC on purchase
  • Exempt: Educational services provided by a school - No GST payable, but school must reverse ITC on inputs used
  • Conditional: Construction of affordable housing (below ₹45 lakhs) - Exempt from GST

Threshold Exemptions

GST provides threshold exemptions to small businesses:

  • Goods: Aggregate turnover up to ₹40 lakhs (₹20 lakhs for special category states)
  • Services: Aggregate turnover up to ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs for special category states)
  • Composition Scheme: Turnover up to ₹1.5 crores with concessional tax rates

Special Category State Exemptions

Higher thresholds are provided for special category states:

  • Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Jammu and Kashmir, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, Tripura, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand
  • Higher exemption limits to promote economic development
  • Special provisions for North Eastern states

Input Tax Credit and Exemptions

The treatment of ITC differs for exempt and nil rated supplies:

  • Nil Rated Supplies: ITC on inputs and input services can be availed
  • Exempt Supplies: ITC on inputs and input services must be reversed
  • Mixed Supplies: Proportionate reversal of ITC required

Conditions for Availing Exemptions

Certain exemptions are subject to conditions:

  • Proper documentation and record maintenance
  • Compliance with specified limits or thresholds
  • Registration requirements for certain exemptions
  • Periodic reporting in GST returns
  • Adherence to specific conditions mentioned in notifications

Recent Exemptions and Amendments

The GST Council has introduced several exemptions over time:

  • Exemptions for COVID-19 related supplies
  • Special exemptions for MSMEs
  • Exemptions for affordable housing projects
  • Exemptions for certain agricultural services

Compliance Requirements

Businesses availing exemptions must comply with:

  1. Maintaining proper records of exempt supplies
  2. Separate accounting for exempt and taxable supplies
  3. Reversal of ITC for inputs used in exempt supplies
  4. Correct reporting in GST returns
  5. Retention of supporting documents

Common Challenges and Issues

Businesses face several challenges with exemptions:

  • Difficulty in classifying supplies as exempt or taxable
  • Complexity in ITC reversal calculations
  • Frequent changes in exemption notifications
  • Documentation requirements for claiming exemptions
  • Audit and compliance issues

Conclusion

Exemptions from GST play a vital role in the tax system by reducing the burden on essential goods and services, promoting social welfare, and supporting specific sectors of the economy. Businesses must understand the different types of exemptions, their conditions, and compliance requirements to ensure proper application and avoid disputes with tax authorities. Proper classification of supplies and correct treatment of input tax credit are crucial for maintaining compliance while optimizing tax liability. Regular updates on exemption notifications and adherence to conditions ensure smooth business operations under the GST regime.
